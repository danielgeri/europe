Europe Family Trip Itinerary

Paris • San Sebastián • Barcelona | August 29 - September 11

Trip Overview

This itinerary is designed for a multi-generational family trip to Paris, San Sebastián, and Barcelona, celebrating a 70th birthday. It aims to balance activities suitable for all ages, including toddlers and seniors, while accommodating diverse food preferences.

Travel Dates

August 29 - September 11

Destinations

Paris, San Sebastián, Barcelona

Family Profile

  • Mother-in-law: 70th birthday, picky eater
  • Sister-in-law's family: Dad (very tasty food, abhors mediocre food), Mom (flexible, likes good food), 4-year-old child, 1-year-old toddler
  • My family: Dad (flexible, likes good food), Mom (likes good food), 1-year-old toddler

Paris: August 29 - September 2

Lodging: 9 Rue Racine, Paris, Île-de-France 75006, France

Friday, August 29: Arrival in Paris

  • Afternoon: Depart SFO to Paris (CDG) via United Airlines (Confirmation: MZXXVE, Departs: 2:40 PM)
  • Evening: Overnight flight

Saturday, August 30: Paris Exploration & Gentle Start

  • Morning (10:25 AM): Arrive at Paris CDG. Clear customs and immigration
  • Late Morning/Early Afternoon: Travel from CDG to hotel (9 Rue Racine). Consider pre-booked private transfer with car seats for toddlers
  • Afternoon: Check into hotel. Allow for rest and nap time for toddlers. Gentle exploration of the immediate neighborhood
Activity Suggestion: Explore Jardin du Luxembourg (very close to your hotel). It has playgrounds, pony rides, and plenty of space for children to run around.
Dining Suggestion: For a casual dinner, consider Breizh Café for sweet and savory crepes, or a Big Mamma Group restaurant like Pink Mamma or Ober Mamma for pizza and pasta.

Sunday, August 31: Iconic Paris & Family Fun

  • Morning: Visit the Eiffel Tower. Enjoy views from Champ de Mars or Trocadéro
  • Lunch: Picnic lunch at Champ de Mars or a casual cafe nearby
  • Afternoon: Bateaux-Mouches (Seine River Cruise). Relaxing way to see Parisian landmarks
Dining Suggestion: Les Enfants Rouges (Marais) offers French-Japanese fusion with a kid-friendly menu, or any Big Mamma Group restaurant for Italian food.

Monday, September 1: Mother-in-Law's 70th Birthday Celebration

  • Morning: Birthday Celebration Activity
  • Option 1: Visit Church of St. Chappelle - breathtaking stained-glass windows
  • Option 2: Explore Montmartre and Sacré-Cœur Basilica
Birthday Lunch: Le Comptoir de la Gastronomie for upscale experience, or Bouillon Chartier for traditional Parisian brasserie.
Birthday Dinner: Le Relais de l'Entrecôte for unique steak frites concept, or Lao Siam for authentic Thai cuisine.

Tuesday, September 2: Departure to San Sebastián

  • Morning: Last Parisian breakfast. Pack and check out
  • 12:11 PM: Depart Paris (CDG) to Hendaye via TGV (Confirmation: RAWQ82)
  • 4:47 PM: Arrive Hendaye
  • Evening: Transfer to San Sebastián via local train

San Sebastián: September 2 - September 7

Lodging: Easo Kalea, 27

Wednesday, September 3: San Sebastián Beaches & Fun

  • Morning: Relax at La Concha Beach - calm, shallow waters perfect for children
  • Afternoon: Visit Monte Igueldo Theme Park - take the funicular for iconic views
Dining: Ambrosio in Plaza de la Constitucion for excellent food where kids can play, or Baluarte Donostia next to a playground.

Thursday, September 4: Aquarium & Old Town Charm

  • Morning: Visit San Sebastian Aquarium - manageable size with underwater tunnel
  • Afternoon: Explore Old Town (Parte Vieja) and Plaza de la Constitución
Dining: Explore pintxos scene in Old Town. Atari Gastroleku for outdoor seating, La Viña for famous Basque cheesecake.

Friday, September 5: Day Trip to Biarritz or Getaria

  • Option 1: Biarritz (France): Chic coastal town with beautiful beaches
  • Option 2: Getaria (Spain): Charming fishing village, birthplace of Balenciaga

Saturday, September 6: Parks & Relaxation

  • Morning: Explore Cristina Enea Park - ponds, ducks, playgrounds
  • Afternoon: Visit Parque Alderdi Eder - playground and old-fashioned carousel
Special Birthday Dinner: Ambrosio for family-friendly atmosphere, or Bodegón Alejandro for traditional Basque cuisine. Don't miss La Viña for Basque cheesecake!

Sunday, September 7: Departure to Barcelona

  • 5:55 PM: Depart San Sebastián (EAS) to Barcelona (BCN) via Vueling (VY2487)
  • 7:00 PM: Arrive Barcelona

Barcelona: September 7 - September 11

Lodging: Avinguda Diagonal, 438

Monday, September 8: Gaudi's Masterpieces & Park Fun

  • Morning: Visit Parc Güell - iconic Gaudi park with unique architecture
  • Afternoon: Visit Sagrada Familia (exterior views)
Dining: Pim Pam Burger/Plats in Born/Picasso Museum Area for good burgers, or explore Gothic Quarter for tapas.

Tuesday, September 9: Beach, Aquarium & Gothic Charm

  • Morning: Enjoy Barceloneta Beach - sandy beach with playground
  • Afternoon: Visit Barcelona Aquarium - one of Europe's largest
  • Evening: Explore Gothic Quarter and Barcelona Cathedral
Dining: Bo de B for healthy Middle Eastern food, or Bliss for outdoor cafe in quiet square.

Wednesday, September 10: Montjuïc & Farewell Dinner

  • Morning: Explore Montjuïc Hill and El Poble Espanyol
  • Afternoon: Relaxed afternoon - revisit favorites or shop for souvenirs
Farewell Dinner: Santa Marta for beach views and relaxed atmosphere, or Pez Vela for elegant grilled fish and meats.

Thursday, September 11: Departure

  • 2:05 PM: Depart Barcelona (BCN) to SFO via Iberia (J5BFD)

Practical Travel Tips & Recommendations

Pace Yourselves

With toddlers and seniors, build in plenty of downtime, including naps. Don't overschedule - it's better to do fewer activities well.

Strollers & Carriers

A compact, foldable stroller is highly recommended. A baby carrier is useful for museums or crowded areas.

Car Seats

For taxis and transfers, especially in Paris, consider pre-booking services that provide car seats. In Spain, car seats are legally required for children under 1.35 meters.

Snacks & Hydration

Always carry plenty of snacks and drinks for toddlers. Carry water bottles and refill frequently, especially important for seniors and children.

Food for Picky Eaters

Look for universal options like pizza, pasta, plain grilled chicken/fish, or fries. In Paris, crepes are good. In San Sebastián, pintxos offer variety in small portions. In Barcelona, tapas work well.

Birthday Celebration

For the mother-in-law's 70th birthday, consider special meals at quality restaurants that are still comfortable for the whole family. Perhaps arrange a cake or small surprise.

Safety & Communication

Be aware of surroundings in crowded tourist areas. Keep open communication within the family about daily plans and preferences.
